Oskar Schindler

Here's a time line of Oskar Schindler's life:

  • 1908 - Born 28 April Zwittau, Moravia, Austria-Hungary
  • 1936 - Worked in several trades until he joined the Abwehr, the intelligence service of Nazi Germany.
  • 1938 - He was arrested for espionage by the Czech government but was released under the terms of the Munich Agreement.
  • 1939 - Schindler acquired an enamelware factory in Kraków, Poland
  • 1944 - Employed about 1,750 workers, of whom 1,000 were Jews at the factory's peak.
  • 1944 - Göth's secretary Mietek Pemper compiled and typed the list of 1,200 Jews who travelled to Brünnlitz in October
  • 1945 - Schindler continued to bribe SS officials to prevent the execution of his workers until the end of World War II in Europe in May.
  • 1945 - Schindler moved to West Germany after the war
  • 1963 - He was named Righteous Among the Nations by the Israeli government.
  • 1974 - He died on 9 October in Hildesheim, Germany, and was buried in Jerusalem on Mount Zion, the only member of the Nazi Party to be honoured in this way.
